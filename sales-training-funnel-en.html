Core Principle: The Only Goal for Sales Is Conversion
  1. Every stage has clear exit criteria — If not met, do not advance to the next stage; if met, you must push forward
  2. Follow-up is systematic, not based on gut feeling — Every touchpoint has a defined frequency, number of attempts, and script
  3. No cherry-picking leads — CRM lead status must be updated in real time and reviewed weekly
  4. Every interaction must have a purpose — Every call, every message should move the client to the next stage
  5. Rejection is not the end — Ask why, address the concern, and reactivate

MQL - Marketing Qualified Lead
A lead that has passed initial screening: has contact info and has named a project of interest. SDR calls to verify contact info and confirm basic interest direction.
Stage Definition & Criteria

Entry Criteria

  • Has reachable contact info (phone / WeChat / email)
  • Has named a specific project or product of interest

Exit Criteria (Exit → SAL)

  • Contact info has been verified as valid (answered / replied)
  • Client's project or direction of interest is known

Value Provided to Client

  • A fast channel to connect with a professional Sales team
  • Notification that their interest has been received and a consultant will reach out
  • Answers to initial simple questions
SDR Outbound Call — Must Be Completed Within 24 Hours

The core action in the MQL stage is the SDR phone call: verify that the contact info works and confirm the client's interest direction. Booking a meeting is the next step in SAL.

  1. Make the first call within 24 hours — No more than 24 hours after MQL entry; no delays
  2. Confirm the client's identity and interest — "We noticed you're interested in [project/listing]; we'd like to learn a bit about your situation"
  3. Collect basic information — What type of home are they interested in? Which area? Owner-occupied or investment?
  4. Verify contact info is valid — Phone is reachable / WeChat responds; record the best contact method and time window

4
SAL - Sales Accepted Lead
Sales officially takes over: schedule a Virtual Consultation or In-person meeting, understand client intent, confirm they are actively looking, and gather detailed needs and financial situation.
Stage Definition & Criteria

Entry Criteria

  • Has expressed interest in a specific project or product
  • Contact info has been verified as valid

Exit Criteria (Exit → SQL)

  • Detailed needs gathered (area / layout / budget / timeline)
  • Financial situation confirmed (Verbal: income / debt / pre-approval)
  • In-person Tour successfully booked
  • Assessed as High Intent to Buy

Value Provided to Client

  • Answer all client concerns, reduce worries
  • Walk through property details in depth
  • Help the client clarify their real needs and priorities
  • Preliminary financial feasibility assessment (no credit pull)
SAL Stage Key Actions
  1. Book a Virtual Consultation or In-person Meeting — "We offer a free 30-minute 1-on-1 consultation with no obligation. What day this week works for you?" → Send Calendly link to confirm time
  2. Understand buying purpose — Primary residence for comfort? Value investment? Commute or school district needs?
  3. Understand buying capacity — Income (W2 / Base / Bonus / RSU), credit score, existing debt/rent, pre-approval status
  4. Understand buying stage — How long have they been looking? Which areas are they familiar with? Working with another agent?
  5. Define property requirements — Must-haves vs. deal breakers, budget range
  6. Introduce team and service model — Team-based service, no commission rebate, requires exclusive partnership
  7. Push to next step — Book a specific date and properties for an In-person Tour
Handling Client Refusal to Advance During 1-on-1
During or after 1-on-1 → Client unwilling to book In-person Tour
"No longer have a need"
→ Ask why: Market changed? Already bought? Budget changed? → Respond accordingly, log in CRM; if truly no need, move to Nurture pool
"Have a need but this one isn't a fit"
→ What doesn't fit? Area? Price? Layout? → Recommend other matching listings → Rebook Tour
"Working with another agent"
→ Did they sign an exclusive? → Showcase team differentiators (team-based service, off-market inventory, investment expertise) → Stay in touch and wait for opportunity
"Want to think about it"
→ Follow up within 48h with matching listings → Try booking a Tour again after 3 days → After two refusals, ask for the reason → Downgrade to Nurture but maintain weekly touchpoints

5
SQL - Sales Qualified Lead
The client has toured a property in person. This is the critical stage where Sales builds trust face-to-face with the client.
Stage Definition & Criteria

Entry Criteria

  • In-person Tour has been booked

Exit Criteria (Exit → Offering)

  • Has completed at least one In-person Tour
  • Client has expressed intent to purchase a specific property
  • Ready to submit an offer or place a deposit

Value Provided to Client

  • Professional in-person walkthrough: market, product, company capabilities
  • On-site experience to eliminate information asymmetry
  • Deep understanding of client preferences for precise matching
  • Build personal trust and rapport
Tour Actions
  1. Pre-tour preparation — Organize listing info, CMA data, and the client's previous requirements. The first tour is especially critical.
  2. Build trust — Face-to-face interaction; demonstrate expertise and team resources. It's not just "showing a house" — it's "consultation + showing."
  3. Deeply understand the client — Observe their reactions to different properties; identify true preferences (what they say vs. how they actually react).
  4. Record Sales Notes — Client's comments on each property, areas of interest, concerns — log in CRM the same day.
  5. Push to next step — On the day the Tour ends, ask: Book another Tour, or get ready to make an offer?
Post-Tour Follow-Up Decision Tree
Same day or next day after Tour → Follow-up contact
Ask: How did you feel about today's properties? Any concerns?
Satisfied, willing to make an offer
→ Immediately enter the Offering process; prepare documents
Satisfied but wants to see more
→ Book the next Tour date; recommend new listings → Stay in SQL stage
Not satisfied
→ Ask specifically what they didn't like:
Price / area / layout not a fit
→ Adjust recommendation strategy; match new listings → Book next Tour
Concerns about the market or timing
→ Use data and case studies for market education (refer to Scripts) → Maintain follow-up
Unhappy with the team or service
→ Address directly; escalate to Team Leader if needed
No response to follow-up
→ Day 2: follow up again → Day 5: send matching listings → Day 7: phone call → 14 days with no response: downgrade back to Nurture

6
Offering
The client has decided to buy this property and begins preparing and submitting an Offer. This is the key moment to demonstrate professionalism.
Stage Definition & Criteria

Entry Criteria

  • Client is ready to buy this property
  • Purchase intent has been confirmed

Exit Criteria (Exit → Reservation)

  • Offer has been formally submitted
  • All documents are complete

Value Provided to Client

  • Professional Disclosure review (first-time Zoom walkthrough)
  • CMA comparable sales analysis to support pricing decisions
  • Complete Offer Package preparation
  • Coordination with Lender to confirm financing
  • Help the client avoid overpaying
Offering Process — Demonstrate Professionalism, Help Close the Deal
  1. Educate the Buyer on required documents — Guide the client on what materials to prepare (POF / Pre-approval / ID documents)
  2. Download and review Disclosures — Share with client; first time must be a Zoom walkthrough (TDS / SPQ / NHD / Inspection / Prelim)
  3. CMA Analysis — Use MLS to research nearby comparable sales; provide data-backed pricing recommendation
  4. Confirm financing with Lender — Especially critical before submitting a non-contingency offer
  5. Prepare Offer Package — RPA, Cover Letter, Disclosure Coversheet, Pre-approval / POF
  6. Submit Offer — Confirm Listing Agent (LA) received and acknowledged

Make Reservation
Pre-sale property: the client places a fully refundable deposit to lock in the unit.
Stage Definition & Criteria

Entry Criteria

  • Buyer has submitted an Offer
  • Offer was accepted or pre-sale unit was selected

Exit Criteria (Exit → In Contract)

  • Deposit has been received
  • Reservation documents have been signed

Value Provided to Client

  • The property is locked in for the client
  • Deposit is fully refundable, reducing client's perceived risk
  • Assist with the deposit process
Actions
  1. Process the Deposit — Guide the client through wire transfer; confirm Escrow has received funds
  2. Sign Reservation documents — Ensure all paperwork is complete
  3. Notify all parties — Confirm with Listing Agent; update status internally
  4. Prepare for In Contract stage — Connect with Lender to begin the loan process

In Contract
Formal contract stage. The property is ready to buy; execute loan, title transfer, and all transaction processes.
Stage Definition & Criteria

Entry Criteria

  • The client's reserved property enters formal transaction
  • Property is fully ready to buy

Exit Criteria (Exit → Close)

  • Escrow has received all funds
  • Loan is Final Approved & Funded
  • All documents have been signed
  • County Recording is complete

Value Provided to Client

  • End-to-end transaction management so the client can relax
  • Timeline monitoring to never miss a deadline
  • Coordinate Lender / Escrow / Listing Agent across all parties
  • Handle insurance, appraisal, repairs, etc. for the client
Transaction Management Process
  1. Sign all Disclosures
  2. Set up & monitor Timeline — Schedule for all milestone dates
  3. Submit EMD (Earnest Money Deposit) — Follow up on Escrow Wire Instructions; confirm receipt
  4. Confirm loan execution — Coordinate with Lender; follow up on Appraisal
  5. Purchase homeowner's insurance — Bind to the loan
  6. Handle Home Warranty
  7. Inspection follow-up — If repairs are needed, negotiate credit with the seller
  8. Utility Transfer List — Send to client 2 days before Close
  9. Final Walkthrough
  10. Notary Signature → Funding → Recording

Close Won / Close Lost
Transaction complete. Won = successfully closed. Lost = dropped out at any stage.
Close Won

Completion Indicators

  • County Recording is complete
  • Grant Deed has been issued
  • All funds have been disbursed

After-Close Actions

  • Send Closing documents to client within 2 business days
  • Confirm the client received the Closing Package
  • Ask for a Google Review
  • Close Gift (owner-occupied: gift box / gift card up to $200)
  • Send referral bonus to the referring party
  • Set a Calendar reminder: anniversary check-in at 11 months

Absolute Hard Rules — Every Sales Rep Must Follow:
1. First contact must be made within 24 hours of MQL entry — no exceptions
2. Follow-up must happen within 24 hours after a Tour — no exceptions
3. Every contact must be logged in CRM — no exceptions
4. Every lead must have a defined next action step — never "wait for the client to contact me"
5. Close Lost must have a documented reason — never just mark it Lost and move on
