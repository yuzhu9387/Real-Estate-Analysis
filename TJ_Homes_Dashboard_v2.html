Section 1: Purchase Strategy & Parcel Profile

Land acquisition patterns across 111 parcels in Bay Area submarkets

Key Insights:
  • San Jose dominates with 42 properties (38%), followed by Menlo Park (15) and Redwood City (11).
  • TJ Homes targets lots averaging 6,000–8,000 SF in most cities — large enough for 2,800+ SF new builds.
  • Median purchase price ranges from ~$1.2M (San Jose) to $3.5M+ (Atherton, Los Altos Hills) — showing a deliberate tiered market strategy.
  • Most parcels have existing homes built 1940–1960, confirming a teardown-and-rebuild model targeting aging housing stock.
  • Common lot dimensions cluster around 50×100 ft and 60×120 ft — indicating a preference for rectangular, buildable lots.

Section 2: Plan & Design Strategy

New construction specifications and floorplan library analysis

Key Insights:
  • Average new build is ~3,200 SF — significantly larger than tear-down originals (~1,400 SF avg).
  • Living-to-lot ratio averages ~0.48, meaning TJ builds roughly half the lot as living space — maximizing zoning allowances.
  • Standard configuration: 4-5 beds, 4-5 baths, 2-car garage — targeting premium family buyers.
  • Only 4 out of 41 floorplan signatures repeat — TJ creates mostly custom plans per parcel rather than cookie-cutter.
  • ADU presence varies by city — higher in San Jose and Redwood City where lot sizes and zoning allow accessory units.

Section 3: Permit & Construction Timeline

Speed metrics from purchase to listing and listing to completion/sale

Key Insights:
  • Average time from purchase to first listing is highly variable — San Jose averages ~230 days, while Menlo Park averages ~50 days.
  • Faster listing in higher-priced markets suggests pre-development planning and expedited permitting in premium areas.
  • Listing-to-completion/sale averages 250–400+ days depending on city — construction cycle of 8–14 months.
  • Menlo Park shows longest listing-to-completion (~406 days avg) — possibly larger/more complex builds for ultra-premium market.
  • Cupertino and Mountain View show faster timelines — possibly smaller lot builds with streamlined permitting.

Section 4: Sales & Profitability

44 properties with sales data — margin, profit, and absorption speed analysis

Key Insights:
  • Gross margins average ~40–55% in most cities, indicating strong value creation through teardown-rebuild.
  • Estimated net profit (after $500/sf construction + 4% closing) ranges widely — highest in premium markets like Atherton and Los Altos Hills.
  • Presale strategy: most properties are listed during construction ("In Construction" stage) — TJ sells before completion to reduce holding risk.
  • Absorption speed (listing to contract) averages 30–90 days in most markets — fast turnover for luxury new construction.
  • Cities with higher margins tend to have longer timelines — a classic risk/return trade-off across submarkets.
